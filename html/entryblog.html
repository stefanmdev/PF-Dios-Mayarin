HOW TO SERVE A BEER

To savor and enjoy a beer in all its fullness, it is very important to know how to serve it. Although more than how to serve a beer, we must talk about how to "throw" a beer, an art that is synonymous with professional quality. The answer is very simple: with one or two foam fingers. The foam protects the liquid and prevents it from oxidizing, also helping to retain and highlight the aromas and taste, something that was achieved in the past by using lids on the jars. Foam is a good sign that the beer is well stabilized and that the fermentation process has been completed correctly. A good fermentation must break down the sugars and starch into alcohol and carbon dioxide that, when released, must form the foam that should ideally be bright and last over time before fading, as well as leaving its mark in the form of rings on the walls of the bowl. Although ceramic tankards are pieces highly valued by collectors and many people drink beer from these containers, it is advisable to use a glass one, the finer the better. The glass will allow us to better appreciate the transparency of the beer, its color, its aroma and even its smell. Of the glass containers, the glass is, without a doubt, the best since it can be held by the foot, thus preventing the beer from heating up.
